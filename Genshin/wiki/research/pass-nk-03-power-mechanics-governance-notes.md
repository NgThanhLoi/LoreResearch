# Nod-Krai Power & Mechanics Governance Notes (Pass NK-03)

This document establishes the source-status-explicit governance notes for power systems, artifacts, and natural mechanics in the Nod-Krai region, strictly grounded in verified local sources.

---

## 1. Verified & Supported Mechanics

### Kuuvahki

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0009`, `AQ-NK-0001`
*   **reliability**: High (Books), Medium (Quest Summary)
*   **what is supported**: A primordial energy of the moon belonging to the Light Realm (referred to by Yohualtecuhtin). It is pre-elemental and shares characteristics with Phlogiston in Natlan and Primordial Sea water in Fontaine. Shards of the Eternal and Iridescent Moons that fell on Nod-Krai are the source of this energy. Aino utilizes this energy in Nasha Town to power mechanical robots and the Kuuvahki Cannon.
*   **what is not supported**: It does not require a Vision to manifest and cannot be channeled via standard modern element-manipulation arts unless converted.
*   **safe usage**: Describing it as a local moon-derived energy comparison to Phlogiston.
*   **unsafe usage**: Speculating on its interaction with the Abyss or using it to invent new elemental systems.
*   **notes**: First introduced and analyzed in Archon Quest Chapter V Act I.

### Moon Marrow

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0009`, `AQ-NK-0001`
*   **reliability**: High (Books), Medium (Quest Summary)
*   **what is supported**: A sacred relic belonging to the Frostmoon Scions. It is used in religious ritual ceremonies when the moon draws closest to Teyvat. Stolen historically by the fae Governor Trubetskoy, and stolen in the present day by the Traveler's Sibling using Abyssal energy. After a confrontation with Sandrone, it was thrown back and is currently stored inside the Traveler's body.
*   **what is not supported**: Detailed magical activation laws or cosmic power descriptions are absent from local files.
*   **safe usage**: Referencing it as a stolen Scion relic now housed in the Traveler.
*   **unsafe usage**: Assigning it Archon-level cosmic powers.
*   **notes**: A key narrative driver of AQ Chapter V Act I.

### Varcolac / Volkodlak Shapeshifting

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0005`, `BK-0007`
*   **reliability**: High
*   **what is supported**: The physiological ability of the Varcolac to shift between human and massive wolf-like forms. Anatomically, they possess two hearts: a human heart and a fae heart. If their human heart (referred to as the "extraneous heart") is killed or pierced, they do not die; instead, they become "complete," unlocking their full wolf-beast form and strength.
*   **what is not supported**: Any other elemental or magical shape-shifting properties.
*   **safe usage**: Explaining the dual-heart physiology and its combat implications.
*   **unsafe usage**: Creating arbitrary werewolf sub-species or new powers.
*   **notes**: Outlined in the book *Night Murmurs From the Far Border*.

### Fae Calendar Cycle

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0003`, `BK-0005`
*   **reliability**: High
*   **what is supported**: A Snezhnayan pre-history temporal metric where one cycle equals exactly 7 years. The founding of Kitezh (4th year of the 417th Cycle) and the coronation of Tsaritsa Anastasya (1st year of the 799th Cycle) are measured using this system. The calendar is deduced to have begun approximately 6,100 years ago (close to the Funerary Year).
*   **what is not supported**: Alignment with current Teyvat celestial seasons outside Snezhnaya.
*   **safe usage**: Chronologically mapping pre-history events.
*   **unsafe usage**: Applying the cycle to date modern quest lines.
*   **notes**: Confirms that Snezhnayan pre-history dates back thousands of years.

### Frostlamp Flowers

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0007`
*   **reliability**: High
*   **what is supported**: A local flora that emits a blood-like red light. They grow buried under snow. When snow is suspended in midair, they are revealed, and their light can stream towards wounded Varcolac shapeshifters, gradually healing their wounds.
*   **what is not supported**: Direct utility as Cryo or Pyro catalysts.
*   **safe usage**: Describing them as werewolf-healing flora.
*   **unsafe usage**: Listing them as standard potion ingredients for non-werewolf characters.
*   **notes**: Outlined in *Night Murmurs From the Far Border*.

## 2. Blocked & Unsupported Mechanics

*   **False Sky Soul Recycling**: Theories suggesting Celestia harvests or recycles human souls through the false sky are unverified.
*   **True Welkin Moon Mechanics**: Claims regarding the Welkin Moon thăng hoa or "Tri-Lunar Authority" are blocked.
*   **Dottore False Moon**: Claims that Dottore built a false moon god or hacked Irminsul are unverified.
*   **Columbina Ascension**: Claims that Columbina ascends to the Welkin Moon are blocked.
*   **Skirk / Third Descender Identity**: Claims regarding Skirk's teachings or the Third Descender's identity remain blocked due to a total lack of local source text.
