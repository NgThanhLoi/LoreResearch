# Nod-Krai Faction Governance Notes (Pass NK-03)

This document establishes the source-status-explicit faction profiles for the groups operating within or historical to the Nod-Krai region, based strictly on registered local sources.

---

## 1. Frostmoon Scions

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0003`, `BK-0007`, `BK-0009`, `AQ-NK-0001`
*   **source_type**: Book, Quest Summary
*   **reliability**: High (Books), Medium (Quest Summary)
*   **confirmed scope**: A localized clan of Snezhnaya, residing at the Frostmoon Enclave on Hiisi Island. They trace their ancestry to the ancient lost realm of Hyperborea and worship the Goddess of the Frost Moon (Huurrekuutar).
*   **safe claims**:
    *   Traces roots to the Golden Realm of Hyperborea after its destruction.
    *   Led by Moonchanter Lauma in the present day.
    *   Historically established by Aila, the first Moonchanter who codified the ancient hymns, and her sister Alia.
    *   Possess and protect the sacred relic **Moon Marrow**, used during key lunar events.
    *   Use pre-elemental frost moon magic (antecedent to Cryo) without requiring a Vision. Some members possess physiological traits like antlers/horns on their heads.
    *   Vilemina was a member who betrayed the clan to the Fatui, subsequently severing her own antlers in exile.
*   **guarded claims**:
    *   The link between the deity Kuutar and Harbinger Columbina must remain classified as a local myth or character theory only. It is *not* confirmed narrator canon.
*   **banned_use**: Writing any storyline details beyond Archon Quest Chapter V Act I, or creating complex organizational hierarchies.
*   **related terms**: Hyperborea, Frost Moon, Huurrekuutar, Moon Marrow, Kuuvahki, Aila, Alia, Lauma, Vilemina.
*   **related characters/entities**: Lauma (Moonchanter), Vilemina (exiled member), Columbina (associated via the Kuutar legend).
*   **notes**: Character-specific character sheet work is deferred to the Full Character Foundation Plan.

## 2. Lightkeepers

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0008`
*   **source_type**: Book
*   **reliability**: High
*   **confirmed scope**: A historic frontier organization operating at the northernmost borders of Nod-Krai, tasked with lighting beacons to ward off Abyss monsters ("Wild Hunt") and protecting the living.
*   **safe claims**:
    *   Historically founded by the Snezhnayan nobleman Silvestr Petrovich Solovei (known as "The Nightingale"), who lit the first beacon upon the summit in Piramida to dispel the "delirium."
    *   Guided by the oath: *"Our bones and blood we shall use as fuel, we shall make life greater than death."*
    *   Sergei Semyonovich Mayakov was a past leader who perished in an unequal duel when a Lighthouse fell, failing to send a distress signal and losing his men.
    *   Kyryll Chudomirovich Flins (known as "Lantern Fae" or "Ratnik Flins") is a modern member who maintains the Oath Lanterns and guards the northern cemeteries.
*   **guarded claims**:
    *   Claims identifying Rotwang's devices with the folklore "Sampo Mill" are unverified and guarded.
*   **banned_use**: Claiming the Lightkeepers have a large active military force in Snezhnaya, or linking them to modern Fatui operations.
*   **related terms**: Piramida, Kofitse, Silvestr Petrovich Solovei, Sergei Semyonovich Mayakov, Ratnik.
*   **related characters/entities**: Silvestr Solovei, Sergei Mayakov, Flins.
*   **notes**: Modern wild animals fear Flins, hinting at a hidden nature, but this nature remains unrevealed.

## 3. Fatui / Oprichniki / Early Fatui Origin

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0005`, `BK-0009`, `AQ-NK-0001`
*   **source_type**: Book, Quest Summary
*   **reliability**: High (Books), Medium (Quest Summary)
*   **confirmed scope**: The Snezhnayan military and political organization.
*   **safe claims**:
    *   Historically established by Tsaritsa Anastasya Feodorovna in Snezhnograd in the 3rd year of the 799th Cycle, replacing the traditional Druzhna army to suppress fae aristocrats.
    *   Built their primary fortress, Glupov ("city of fools"), in the 4th year of the 799th Cycle.
    *   Historically utilized the **Oprichniki** (secret police force).
    *   In modern Nod-Krai, they established the **Kuuvahki Experimental Design Bureau** on Paha Island to study and extract lunar energy from moon shards.
    *   Harbinger Sandrone (Marionette) is active in Nod-Krai, seeking the Moon Marrow relic. She briefly threw the relic to the Traveler during a Wild Hunt assault.
*   **guarded claims**:
    *   Sandrone's alleged links to Rotwang or Khaenri'ah are unverified and blocked.
*   **banned_use**: Integrating Dottore's false moon god or hacking of Irminsul as canon.
*   **related terms**: Glupov, Tsaritsa Anastasya, Oprichniki, Kuuvahki Experimental Design Bureau, Paha Island.
*   **related characters/entities**: Tsaritsa Anastasya (historical founder), Sandrone (Marionette).
*   **notes**: Harbingers must be referred to only in minimal, source-status-explicit roles.

## 4. Volkodlak / Varcolac

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0005`, `BK-0007`
*   **source_type**: Book
*   **reliability**: High
*   **confirmed scope**: A race of fae shapeshifters (werewolves) in Snezhnayan pre-history.
*   **safe claims**:
    *   Anatomically possess two hearts (one human, one fae). They can shapeshift between human and massive wolf-like beast forms.
    *   Their hair color in human form matches their fur color in beast form (e.g. jet-black).
    *   If their human heart (extraneous heart) is pierced/killed, they do not die but become "complete," permanently unlocking their full beast power.
    *   Have a biological craving for human blood; historically kept servants in estates for bloodletting.
    *   Wounds can be healed by the light of **Frostlamp Flowers** (which are revealed when snow is suspended in midair).
    *   Led in pre-history by King Miscislav, who fought Emperor Monomakh Snezhnyi and was slain by the Emperor's spear.
*   **guarded claims**:
    *   Rumors that Snezhnayan Duchess Lady Evlampiya Orlova is a Varcolac must remain classified as an unverified local myth.
*   **banned_use**: Devising new magical elements or werewolf abilities not recorded in the texts.
*   **related terms**: King Miscislav, Czcibor, Viliana, Frostlamp Flowers.
*   **related characters/entities**: King Miscislav (historical), Lady Evlampiya Orlova (rumored).
*   **notes**: The wolf form of King Miscislav was bear-like/mountainous ("Gorynishche"), and his skull is housed in the Zapolyarny Palace Treasury.

## 6. Reed Miller Folklore Network

*   **source_status**: `local_source_found`
*   **source_ids**: `BK-0004`, `BK-0009`
*   **source_type**: Book
*   **reliability**: High (for folklore/pulp context)
*   **confirmed scope**: The literary network surrounding the legendary thief Reed Miller.
*   **safe claims**:
    *   Reed Miller is a legendary figure in Snezhnayan pulp novels, famous for stealing the **Sampo Mill** from the fae Governor Trubetskoy in Piramida.
    *   The stories feature his lavender-haired female rival/accomplice **Wagtail** (codename "A"), and his lover **Brahe** (who in real history was the adopted human son of Governor Trubetskoy).
    *   In historical reality, the second Reed Miller was a woman named **Alia**, who was loved by Brahe.
    *   The stories include elements of local folklore like Jack Frost and Snegurochka.
*   **guarded claims**:
    *   Treating the Sampo Mill machine in the stories as a real Khaenri'ahn device or a literal historical artifact.
*   **banned_use**: Confusing the pulp stories with literal historical canon.
*   **related terms**: Sampo Mill, Wagtail, Alia, Brahe, Governor Trubetskoy, Leonita.
*   **related characters/entities**: Reed Miller, Alia, Brahe, Trubetskoy.
*   **notes**: This is folklore, though loosely inspired by historical figures like Alia and Brahe.
