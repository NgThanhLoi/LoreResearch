## Summary

Ganyu is the General Secretary of the Liyue Qixing's Yuehai Pavilion, a half-Qilin adeptus who has served under a millennia-old contract with Rex Lapis. Her voice lines establish her as diligent, self-effacing, and socially reserved, with a strict vegetarian discipline tied to her Qilin heritage. She occupies a liminal position between adeptal and human worlds, expressed through quiet devotion to administrative work and cautious interpersonal warmth. Deeper backstory claims (Archon War participation, childhood details) remain source-light in this pass.

## Genre Usage Notes

### Canon-compliant
Use Ganyu as an administrative anchor for Liyue governance scenes, a bridge character between adeptal and human worlds, or a quiet observer of social life.

### AU
AU changes should preserve her core reserve, devotion to duty, and vegetarian discipline unless the AU explicitly alters her heritage.

### Romance
Keep her hesitant, earnest, and slow to open up. Treat vulnerability about her horn and identity as high-trust reveals, not casual flirtation.

### Mystery / Detective
She can provide administrative records, notice procedural irregularities, or recall historical precedents, but should not become an action investigator.

### Political / Faction
Use her as the institutional memory of Liyue governance, observing Qixing dynamics from a neutral-supportive position.

### Action / Battle
Reference dormant adeptal power cautiously. She fights when necessary but prefers peaceful resolution. Do not write extended combat without source support.

### Comedy / Slice of life
Social awkwardness, nap incidents, food temptation resistance, and horn-concealment mishaps work naturally.

### Dark fantasy
Let her ancient perspective and isolation be tested without breaking her core gentleness immediately.

### OC-insert
She can process an OC into Liyue systems or observe them from administrative distance before warming up slowly.

### Livestream / Heavenscreen / Reaction
She reacts with quiet concern, professional composure, and occasional flustered stammering when caught off-guard.
