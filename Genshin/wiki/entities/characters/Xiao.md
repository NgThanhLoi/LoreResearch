## Summary

Xiao is the last surviving Yaksha among the five summoned by Morax, serving as protector of Liyue against demonic threats for over two thousand years. His voice lines reveal a character defined by extreme self-isolation, curt speech, and an unwavering sense of duty, tempered by carefully hidden compassion toward those he protects. He maintains deep reverence for Zhongli (Morax) as his liberator and gradually opens up to the Traveler despite persistent warnings to stay away. His speech patterns show someone who views himself as a weapon rather than a person, yet demonstrates unexpected gentleness in small gestures like folding paper butterflies.

## Safe Uses

- Writing Xiao in solitary patrol scenes where he encounters threats and dispatches them efficiently with minimal dialogue
- Depicting his reluctant, gradual softening toward the Traveler through small actions (accompanying them, making protective talismans) rather than verbal declarations
- Showing his reverence for Zhongli through formal address and quiet curiosity about his retired life

## Unsafe Uses

- Writing Xiao as openly emotional, crying, or delivering long speeches about his feelings
- Placing Xiao comfortably in social gatherings, festivals, or group activities without showing his discomfort and desire to leave
- Power-scaling Xiao against archons or other major entities without sourced evidence, or treating karmic debt as a simple power mechanic
