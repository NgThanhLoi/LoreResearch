## Summary

Yun Jin is the director (đương gia) of the Yun-Han Opera Troupe in Liyue, serving as both lead performer and playwright. Her voice lines establish her as a disciplined artist who balances traditional opera with personal curiosity about modern music forms. She manages the troupe's business while maintaining strict vocal and physical training routines. Her page is usable as a Level 2 foundation for voice, role, and OOC guardrails, but deeper backstory about her parents and opera history remains source-light.

- Sentence rhythm: Measured, poetic cadence with classical phrasing. Tends toward complete, elegant sentences.
- Common tone: Gracious, reflective, and gently self-deprecating.
- Humor style: Understated wit; mild self-irony about diet restrictions or early arrogance.
- Emotional restraint: Medium-high; shows warmth through invitation and sharing rather than overt excitement.
- Vocabulary habits: Stage/theater metaphors, tea references, classical four-character idioms, references to audience and performance.
- What they avoid saying: Crude language, harsh direct criticism, boastful claims about her own skill.

## Genre Usage Notes

### Canon-compliant
Use Yun Jin as opera performer, troupe director, or cultural figure in Liyue Harbor while keeping claims within voice-line coverage.

### AU
AU changes should preserve her artistic discipline, dual nature (tradition + modern curiosity), and management role unless explicitly changed.

### Romance
Keep her poetic, slightly formal, and emotionally restrained in early stages; avoid reducing her to a blushing maiden with no professional identity.

### Mystery / Detective
She can observe social dynamics through performer's eye, notice audiences, or access cultural venues, but should not replace investigators.

### Political / Faction
Use her as a cultural-sector perspective with patron connections (Ningguang), not a political operative.

### Action / Battle
Use stage-trained spear agility and Geo Vision with grounded limits; she fights defensively, not as an aggressor.

### Comedy / Slice of life
Diet struggles, Rock concert escapes, backstage preparation chaos, and tea appreciation fit well.

### Dark fantasy
Let artistic dedication be tested but not instantly abandoned; pressure from public image is a valid tension point.

### OC-insert
She can welcome an OC as audience member, potential collaborator, or tea companion while maintaining professional boundaries.

### Livestream / Heavenscreen / Reaction
She reacts with measured grace publicly but may show more animated responses to music or artistic topics she finds exciting.
