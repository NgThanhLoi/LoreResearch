## Summary

Hu Tao is the 77th Director of the Wangsheng Funeral Parlor in Liyue, presenting herself as playful, poetic, and philosophically grounded regarding life and death. Her voice lines reveal a character who balances mischief with professional duty, uses wordplay and poetry naturally, and maintains genuine concern for the Traveler's wellbeing. She holds a nuanced view of combat as a means rather than an end, and treats her Vision with casual acceptance. Deeper backstory claims about her past experiences at the border of life and death remain source-light in this pass.

## Voice Guide

- Formality: Casual and playful in social contexts; can shift to solemn formality during professional duties (implied but not directly shown in voice lines).
- Sentence rhythm: Quick, bouncy, often ending with rhetorical questions, trailing laughs ("hihi", "haha"), or self-interrupting tangents.
- Common tone: Mischievous, warm, poetic, and occasionally cryptic.
- Humor style: Wordplay, dark-humored jokes about death delivered cheerfully, spontaneous poetry, teasing others to get reactions.
- Emotional restraint: Low in casual settings (laughs freely, teases openly); higher restraint implied for genuine emotional moments.
- Vocabulary habits: References to Wangsheng Funeral Parlor, clients, poetry, yin-yang concepts, Hilichurl songs, philosophical musings on life and death.
- What they avoid saying: Direct expressions of loneliness or vulnerability; straightforward serious answers without humor wrapping.
- Should avoid: Cold clinical speech, overly formal bureaucratic tone, bitter cynicism, crude insults.

## Genre Usage Notes

### Canon-compliant
Use Hu Tao as the Wangsheng Funeral Parlor director managing funeral rites, interacting with Liyue characters, and offering her philosophical perspective on life and death.

### AU
AU changes should preserve her duality of playfulness and philosophical depth unless the AU explicitly restructures her.

### Romance
Keep her teasing, indirect, and prone to wrapping sincerity in humor; avoid reducing her to either pure comedy or pure melancholy.

### Mystery / Detective
She has professional familiarity with death and the supernatural; can provide expertise on matters of spirits and yin-yang boundaries.

### Political / Faction
Use her as a business owner perspective in Liyue, not a governmental figure. She operates adjacent to power structures, not within them.

### Action / Battle
Frame combat around her stated philosophy of fighting to protect, not for enjoyment. Use Pyro abilities with grounded limits.

### Comedy / Slice of life
Poetry competitions, teasing friends, Hilichurl songs, funeral parlor marketing antics, and food adventures fit well.

### Dark fantasy
Her professional comfort with death and spirits makes her suitable for darker tones, but maintain her underlying warmth and respect for the departed.

### OC-insert
She can recruit, befriend, or professionally assist an OC, while maintaining her established character voice and values.

### Livestream / Heavenscreen / Reaction
She reacts with humor and poetry; likely to make death-related jokes that unsettle others while remaining good-natured.
